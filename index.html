About

From D1 athlete to
robotics founder.

I graduated from Davidson College in just three years with degrees in Economics and Spanish — all while playing Division I baseball and working multiple jobs. During my junior year, I watched window washers struggling against strong winds on a swing stage and asked myself: "How can we leverage technology to make this safe and more efficient?"

That question became Lucid Bots. I had never built a robot before. I taught myself mechanical, electrical, and software engineering with a $12 soldering iron while living above a garage. That bright-eyed optimism combined with relentless hard work is how we got here.

Today, I lead multiple ventures at the intersection of robotics, AI, and human potential — and mentor the next generation of founders as Innovator-in-Residence at Davidson College.
