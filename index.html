About

From D1 athlete to
robotics founder.

I finished Davidson College in three years — double major in Economics and Spanish, Division I baseball, three jobs at once. Junior year, I saw window washers getting thrown around by wind on a swing stage and thought: "There has to be a better way."

I'd never built a robot. I taught myself mechanical, electrical, and software engineering from YouTube and textbooks, living above a garage with a $12 soldering iron. That naivety was an advantage — I didn't know what was supposed to be impossible.

Today I run Lucid Bots and Persto, and I'm back at Davidson as Innovator-in-Residence helping students turn their own "there has to be a better way" moments into real companies.
